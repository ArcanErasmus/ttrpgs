The Pariah: You met a disgraced exile, and found in his words and attitudes something that spoke to you. What once seemed true in your religion, society, or family began to appear false the more time you spent with this person, and you quickly learned not to trust everyone you meet—especially among those who would claim to be most deserving of it. The Mystic: You were especially close to a holy person in your community who fundamentally changed your life by opening your eyes to the incredible powers that exist beyond the natural world. Regardless of whether you follow a faith, certain religious artifacts, rituals, and texts played a large part in making you the person you are. You gain access to the Child of the Temple faith trait.
- Awakening and Early Life
    - Awakening in a well-appointed house in [major city], I came to understand the nature of my existence. The world was a vast place built of the changes that people had wrought upon it, layered over and over ad infinitum.
    - After much investigation, I discovered my previous self's place in the world. She had been a well-connected merchant, specializing in the discreet and secure shipment of holy relics, as well as the regular trade of more common consumables that churches go through. However, deeper digging started uncovering worrying signs. She made hefty anonymous donations to several churches, but curiously, not to a single one of the faiths she did business with.
    - I started to notice my reputation among the churches. There were persistent rumors that I was akin to a changeling, an unnatural thing that supposedly replaces people and steals their lives. And it wasn't a new one. My past's dealings had been utterly shafting these churches, and I would bet her savings that not all of the "relics" transported had been real, or perhaps there was some other double dealing going on. I had to maintain the lies, relationships, explain my absences and missing memories. I had to negotiate new deals, keep track of bureaucracies that had existed only partially on recordsheets, and sometimes on secret records kept separate from the others.
    - As the stress mounted, I was often visited by fragments of remembered histories, and visions of arcane workings. Rather than illuminate my past life, or help me integrate to the web I found myself in, they teased me with entirely unrelated possibilities. Another problem to manage, or perhaps an escape route?
    - I couldn't stand it. Being seen as someone I only knew through records and anecdotal comments. Trying to juggle her life, her career, watching the mistakes pile up. I was sick of it, and I was going to rid myself of it. I wasn't cut out for this game, nor could I afford to prop up her deceptions for long. It was time to get out before it ruined me. The visions called to me, and I could almost read them clearly now. A change was happening to me, and I could recognize the symbolism. An escape route, one I would take.
- Independent life
    - I acquired a secondhand spellbook. A scribe's compilation, with all the learnings they had pieced together between their day job. They sold it to me for what was probably too high a sum. I got the sense that this was their retirement, so I didn't feel bad overpaying. The writings were similar to my visions, so I would study them until some breakthrough was reached.
    - It took far less time than I thought. Beholding the magical notations rapidly increased the frequency and clarity of my visions until something shifted into alignment. The strange lines and patterns across my body resonated with an energy, and reshaped. They feel more complete now. I can read the texts now, clear as can be.
    - Withdrawing from my obligations has cost me, and it is about to cost me more. I'm selling off any assets I have, burning the records, and leaving. I have my new calling, and I think I hate my predecessor (a common refrain, I'm taken to understand) for the cage she had constructed around herself before dying. In a sense, however, the cage was entirely of my own make. I could have left at any time, and I'm only just now realizing how easy it is. I wonder, sometimes, what shackles I will encircle my own wrists with through my life. Perhaps I will endeavor to leave behind a similarly complex and modest inheritance, that it might challenge the next one in this body much as this challenged me. Maybe with fewer covert criminality, however. That was a narrowly avoided trap, and it could have been utterly ruinous. I should hope that the gods are appreciative that I have chosen to end the scheming of my prior. Much as I do not enjoy how their followers have treated me, I haven't been wronged enough to warrant slowly but steadily impoverishing them.
    - I've wasted enough time as the servant of the past, and the future is still nearly a century away. This life is mine.
- Academy Training:
    - Spent my past wealth disentangling from my previous self's obligations, and then sold the house to move to [big city]. Paid tuition to [wizarding academy], and continued at it until graduation. Considered advancing into full-time academia, but decided against it. After having a vision of being 80 years older, not really considering the passing of time, I made my mind to pick a more active and exploration-filled career. Plus, the allure of being able to afford more and more extravagant magical artifacts, as well as avoiding falling into an inescapable social web, led me to [the guild]. I don't want to merely handle and study objects that pass through my department's purview. I don't want to build up wealth just to spend it making sure it sustains itself and leaves my next life trapped in a prison of mysteries and politics. I don't want to be chained by the past or the future. I want to become a nexus of capability and power, to understand the mysteries of the world as much as possible in the short century I have, and leave it without making a huge mark.
