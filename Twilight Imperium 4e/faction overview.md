# Faction List
### Arborec, 5B
- Plant faction, ramping growth
- Infantry have production, space docks can't make inf
- Slow and strategic play
- Beginner but not for winning
- Slow without leaders, breakthroughs, or 14pt game

### Argent Flight 4B
- Strong destroyers, wall placement, strong anti-fighter barrage
- Bad at late game voting, better early voting
- Tactical and strong play

### The Barony of Letnev, 3B
- Bad economy/trade, fleet focused
- Intimidating and beefy play
- Beginner but not for winning

### The Clan of Saar 5A
- Nomadic, doesn't need to hold home system, can move docks
- Possibly strongest faction
- Mobile and uncaring play

### The Councel Keleres 5A
- Very powerful breakthrough, rush it ASAP
- Self-contained economy
- Odd and experimental play

### The Crimson Rebellion 7A
- Creates new adjacencies by spawning rifts, ironclad home system defense
- Relies on combat and positioning, benefits from all combats in the game
- Bad eco and leaders, need lots of planning
- Unhinged and oppugnant play

### The Deepwrought Scholarate 6A
- Group hug faction, share techs/eco for benefit, coexist on planets
- Wheeling and dealing, bluffing
- Friendly and cooperative play

### The Embers of Muaat 7B
- Death Star faction, love death stars, produce from death stars
- Can nuke a system, breakthrough lets them birth a mobile planet
- Scary and gimmicky play

### The Emirates of Hacan 2B
- Super commodity faction, trade with no adjacency, trade action cards
- Good if you know how to make deals
- Economic and diplomatic play

### The Empyrean 5B
- Love exploring deep space, can do so from game start
- Make tollways, benefits from lots of neighbors
- Calculating and devious play

### The Federation of Sol 3B
- Strong infantry, extra command tokens, strong commodity, strong capacity
- Simple, versatile
- Flexible and bellicose play

### The Firmament/The Obsidian 9A
- Disregard the imperial throne, acquire schemes
- Build up resources with player interactions in the early game
- Pivotal flip into second stage faction, then puppet other players
- Plotting and intricate play
- NOT for new players

### The Ghosts of Creuss 5B
- Love wormholes, like simpler Crimson Rebellion, flagship is wormhole, free movement tech
- All about movement, decent eco
- Abnormal and tricky play

### Last Bastion 7A
- Few starting resources, but military and production tempo from combat
- Tricky to navigate, strong once they hit their stride, confusing home system
- Heroic and rugged play

### The L1Z1X Mindnet 4B
- Mechazombies, convert captured planets
- Bombard during space combat makes for cheap ground war
- Precise and adaptive play
- Strong eco but bad influence

### The Mahact Gene-Sorcerers 9A
- Steal abilities from players, juggle system activations, mug promissory notes
- Very strong but hated
- Technical and dominant play
- NOT for new players

### The Mentak Coalition 6A
- Steal money and become hated
- Annoying pirates, money makes easy VPs
- Sly and antagonistic play

### The Naalu Collective 5B
- Precogs go first
- Stronger fighters
- Prophetic and swarmy play

### The Naaz-Rokha Alliance 3B
- Love exploration and relics, and can re-explore planets
- Playing solitaire, but if someone messes with you it becomes battletech
- Exploratory and exciting play

### The Nekro Virus 10A
- Don't vote, don't research, just spam command tokens
- All about combat, copy others' techs from fights, hated
- Intricate and mechanical play
- NOT for new players

### The Nomad 4B
- Just a guy, starts with upgradeable flagship
- Lots of agents but few units
- Wealthy and multifaceted play

### The Ral Nel Consortium 4A
- Move structures around, very fast destroyers
- Lots of reach with combat adjacencies
- Speedy and punctual play

### Sardakk N'orr 4A
- +1 on combat rolls, can move ground forces without transports
- Breakthrough gives upgrades from winning combats
- Hard to push for victory
- Grounded and invasive play

### The Titans of Ul 6B
- Awaken and grow units on planets
- Streamlined techs, good structure power, good money power
- Constructive and tough play

### The Universities of Jol-Nar 7B
- Fast tech, bad combat
- Streamlined play but lots of abilities
- Complex and academic play

### The Vuil'raith Cabal 8A
- Public enemy number one, eats other players' ships
- Docks give free movement
- Very strong in the Fracture, free relics and units to eat
- Destructive and cruel play
- NOT for new players

### The Winnu 4A
- Glass cannon, "stupid and boring", very fun
- Stretch thin between Mecatol Rex and home system
- Mecatol Rex Or Bust
- Simple and fragile play

### The Xxcha Kingdom 3B
- The turtle faction that's all about turtling
- Veto agendas, which is also a useful tool for trading
- Take planets diplomatically
- One of the strongest breakthroughs
- Defensive and Political play

### The Yin Brotherhood 2B
- Ground combat faction, also flagship is a huge bomb
- Breakthrough lets them copy abilities from unused factions
- Intrusive and steady play

### The Yssaril Tribes 4B
- All about action cards
- Secretive and Stalling play
